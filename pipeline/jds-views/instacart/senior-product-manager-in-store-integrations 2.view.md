## What the role involves

**Surface:** In-Store Platform — APIs, data pipelines, and foundational integrations powering Caper carts, Foodstorm catering, Carrot Tags, and AI-driven store intelligence

**Business model:** B2B enterprise SaaS / platform — integrations sold/licensed to grocery retailers

**Team size:** 11 direct teammates (majority backend engineers); broader Connected Stores org of a few hundred people

**Cross-functional partners:** Engineering, Partnerships, Enterprise Solutions, Solution Architects, External retail technology providers (POS, loyalty, inventory, coupon system vendors)

**Primary metrics (inferred):** Time-to-integrate; Data coverage and quality; Integration reliability (SLAs, error budgets); Product adoption of integrations
**Core responsibilities:**

- Own the integration roadmap for Connected Stores: strategy, scope, and prioritization based on business impact, data value, and technical feasibility
- Drive end-to-end execution: align Engineering, Partnerships, Solution Architecture, and 3rd party teams from discovery through launch and scale
- Lead external partner alignment with retailers and software vendors; define data contracts and ensure technical fit
- Ship first-of-a-kind integrations including those powering new AI products; document patterns and transition repeatable work to Solution Architects
- Establish SOWs and SLAs; draft requirements, timelines, and deliverables; negotiate partner responsibilities
- Set and measure KPIs such as time-to-integrate, data coverage and quality, reliability, and product adoption
- Resolve conflicts and remove blockers across internal product needs and external constraints
